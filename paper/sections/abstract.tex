\begin{abstract}
Do user-stated quality thresholds (``this only needs to be a 70 out of 100'') actually constrain LLM revision behavior? In a two-turn writing setup with 3,840 trials, we cross follow-up prompt phrasing (2 levels), stated quality threshold (8 levels), and model (Claude Sonnet 4, Gemini 2.5 Flash, GPT-4o) across eight scenarios. Quality thresholds have \textit{no detectable effect} on whether the model revises. A revision-implying follow-up (``Can this be improved?'') triggers near-universal revision (99.9\%) regardless of threshold, while an evaluative follow-up produces 0.3--38.0\% revision rates depending on model. Within triggered revisions, thresholds produce only a weak-to-moderate dose-response (Spearman $\rho = -0.14$ to $-0.50$). These results suggest a two-stage pattern: prompt phrasing controls whether the model revises; the stated threshold modulates revision intensity only after revision is triggered. Under the conditions we tested, threshold-calibration ability goes unused at the revision gate.
\end{abstract}
