\section{Conclusion}

In the two-turn writing-revision setup we tested, the phrasing of the follow-up prompt, not the stated quality threshold, determines whether the model revises. Models possess the ability to calibrate revision intensity to thresholds, but this ability is not activated when the follow-up prompt implies revision.

For alignment research, these findings locate a threshold-blind compliance pattern at a specific decision point where the calibration ability models demonstrate goes unused. Future work should test whether targeted interventions can restore threshold sensitivity at the gate, whether the compliance cliff generalizes beyond writing tasks, and whether human evaluators converge with LLM judges on overcorrection severity.
